Here are different ressources I personally use and enjoy while learning turkish

  • Duolingo:
    It is a great introduction to the language and a simple way to keep studying on a daily basis! It lacks of some fundamentals explanations but will end up teaching you +1k words.
  • Duome:
    It's a great ressource full of tips and notes on what is missing on Duolingo. Overall, a great complement to any study.
  • Grammar books:
    My favorite and most useful book was 45 leçons pour parler turc which explains very clearly many concepts and makes the correct parallel with french language. Many exercises guide the learning.

Lesson 1 - Turkish Vowel Harmony

1. Major Vowel Harmony

Major vowel harmony concerns the frontness or backness of vowels. Turkish vowels are divided into two groups:

  • Back vowels: a, ı, o, u
  • Front vowels: e, i, ö, ü

Rule:

  • If the first vowel in a root word is a back vowel, subsequent vowels will also be back vowels.
  • If the first vowel is a front vowel, subsequent vowels will also be front vowels.

Examples:

  • Back vowels: kalem ("pen") → kalemim ("my pen")
  • Front vowels: köy ("village") → köyüm ("my village")

2. Minor Vowel Harmony

Minor vowel harmony applies to the roundedness and openness of vowels. Vowels are grouped into:

  • Unrounded vowels: a, e, ı, i
  • Rounded vowels: o, ö, u, ü
Rule:
  • A rounded vowel is followed by a rounded vowel or an unrounded close vowel (ı, i).
  • An unrounded vowel is followed by an unrounded vowel.
Examples:
  • Rounded vowel: yüz ("face") → yüzüm ("my face")
  • Unrounded vowel: ev ("house") → evim ("my house")
