Kayki and I, up to stuff.

Compilation of all our Michelin experiences.

2026/06/25 - Mastard, in Montréal, QC.

First experience together. Mastard is one of the first 1 * restaurant in Québec.
The concept of this restaurant is to offer an original carte blanche menu centered on produce from the terroir, often elevated by sauces, sabayon sauces and emulsions.
The following is the 5 services menu with wine pairing.

Amuse-bouches



It all started with some surprises amuse-bouches. The mignardise one was a sort of spicy cake (with a bit of cinnamon but G still liked it!). And there was a soup served with it, made of pork bones and garlic concentrate. Very powerful in taste and great start.

Scallop | asparagus | lovage



Served with Moravie [CZ], Sauvignon 2023. Already one of our favorite meal. Most fresh and tasty scallop every eaten, it felt it was eaten fresh from the sea. The asparagus was also amazingly prepared, and the green sauce everything was lying down on kept elevating the taste. There was a mini bite of rabbit, which felt very much like a sausage made of multiple spices (K was a bit suspicious of the rabbit and gave away half of it). On top of everything, the meal was served with bread from Automne (K loves bread), and their persil butter was to fall for.

Lobster | tomato | pork



Served with Latium [IT], Ribelà bianco 2024. Great follow-up. We like that this menu was more fish-oriented. We see here all the work put into the emulsions. The onion sparks made it very fun to take mini-bites after mini-bites. And the lobster was soooo good! The orange wine chosen was very funky and fitted well with the meal.

Special addition made of tuna



Not on the official menu, we shared the special of the day which was for both of us the peak of the experience. The tuna was very slowly cooked on only one face, making it one of the best tuna ever eaten. There is also a concentrate of zuchinnis with mint on the side which particularly fun to eat.

Swordfish | spinach | parsley



Served with Rheinhessen [DEU], Weiss 2022. The swordfish was good. There is next to it this sausage of spinash which really felt like a condensed version of spinash, not bad but very overpowering. Also, the very green sauce next to it? Kale; which made elements taste like green smoothies. But the mix of sauces on the side was, one again, delicious.

Guinea fowl | ramp | tokyo turnip



Served with Côtes du Rhône [FR], L'Art d'Être Heureux 2024. At this point of the meal, we were dying. The guinea fowl was great with its skin on top. But, following the previous plate we just had, there was a bit of a repetition of earthy flavors. We felt like, each meal was mastering different savors and ingredients, and that it was more made to be tasted one bite after the other. But the long sequence of terroir tastes made us miss some funky spices.

Strawberry | mint | vanilla



Served with Württemberg [DEU], Riesling Kabinett 2024. After being too full from the meal, the dessert actually felt relieving. Everything was very fresh and flowing well, from the vanilla concentrate to the ice cream, and even the rug of homemade caramel under.

Last bites



The white one was a sort of riz au lait, a bit warm and unexpected. The jelly one had a funny texture, very close from a pate de fruit, rhubarb taste. Finally, we both finished on, and loved, the chocolate one, melting in the middle, and leaving a taste that remains for a while.

K final word: "Earthy".
G final word: "Quebecy"
